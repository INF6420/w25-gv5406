About Me

I obtained my undergraduate degree at Albion College, where I graduated with Honors with a major in History and a minor in Art History. While I was at Albion, I participated in numerous extracurriculars such as the Prentiss M. Brown Honors Program, the Substance-Free Intentional Learning Community, and the Albion College Model United Nations Club, where I served as President. While at Albion College, I utilized the college's resources to develop my professionally. I obtained an internship at the Detroit Free Press through the college's Institute for Public Policy, where I became familiar with the editorial process as I managed the "Letters to the Editor" column. Later, I spent a semester working in the college's archives, where I learned basic archival practices and assisted in cataloging the filing cabinets in the archive with Archivists' Toolkit.

In addition to the skills I developed in college, I have obtained extensive customer service experience in a wide variety of settings. Whether it be in an academic library, a grocery store, or a fine dining restaurant, I strive to be a positive force in my interactions with coworkers and customers. Along with customer service, I also have extensive experience with inventory management, which I credit with working in retail. While inventory management is very important to learn when you're working in a library, I've noticed that it is most needed when the inventory that you're managing is perishable.
